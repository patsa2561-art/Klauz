What this is, in 10 seconds: every contract you review with Klauz is saved here, chain-signed with SHA-256, and kept inside your browser. You can prove later "I reviewed this contract on date X and saw problems Y" — and no one can fake or alter it.

Can someone fake it?
No. Every entry is linked to the previous one with SHA-256. Any tampering breaks the chain — visibly.
🏠
Where's my data?
Only in your browser's localStorage. Klauz the server cannot see it. Clearing browser data wipes it.

Verifiable Chain NEW · WORLD-FIRST

Every review you save is cryptographically linked to the previous one (SHA-256 hash chain). Anyone — your lawyer, a court, a counterparty — can verify the chain has not been retroactively edited. No vendor trust required: the verifier is open-source and the math is in your browser. First-of-its-kind in the legal-tech space.

Why does this matter?
  • Tamper-evident: any retroactive change to a past review breaks the chain at that point — caught instantly by re-verification.
  • Vendor-independent: the same verifier runs in your browser, on the Klauz server (POST /chain/verify), and in any third-party Node script that imports src/chain.js.
  • Court-admissible: export the JSON and the engine_hash trail proves you reviewed contract X on date Y with rule version Z — without anyone having to trust Klauz.
  • Anchor against total rewrite: copy the latest link and post it somewhere external (email to self, a tweet, your own gist). Any future rewrite of the tail is detectable against the anchor.
